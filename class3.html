The Basics of Lightning Talks

Lightning Talks Defined

Short, 5-10 minute talks.
Often, several will be delivered in a single session.

PechaKucha (20 slides, 20 seconds each) and
Ignite (20 slides, 15 seconds each) are similar formats

Lightning Talks Why

"The goal of lightning talks is to articulate a topic
in a quick, insightful, and clear manner.

These concise and efficient talks are intended to
grab the attention of the audience, convey key information, and
allow for several presenters to share their ideas in a brief period of time."

Wikipedia

Lightning Talks Why Give One

  • Great for new speakers.
  • Great for practicing overcoming nerves.
  • Great for guaging interest in topics.
  • Great for exploring an idea you want to dive deeper on.
  • Great for generating interest in your full talk.

Lightning Talks Big Picture

"People want to discover something new,
not everything there is to know about it."

source

Lightning Talks Focus

Limited time means getting to the point quickly and clearly.

Cut out as much non-critical information as possible

Don't sweat the details. Don't try to cover too much ground.

Lightning Talks Practice...

Practice... Practice...

The only way to be sure you'll stay within the allotted time is to practice and fine tune.

Lightning Talks Topics

"A good lightning talk is insightful, inspiring, thought-provoking, useful, humorous, controversial, or enlightening."

source

Lightning Talks IT Topics

  • Why X is my favorite thing.
  • I want to do cool project X. Who wants to help?
  • Successful Project: I did project X. It was a success. Here's how you could benefit.
  • Failed Project: I did project X. It was a failure, and here's why.
  • Heresy: People always say X, but they're wrong. Here's why.
  • You All Suck: Here's what is wrong with our community.
  • Call to Action: Let's all do more of X / less of X.
  • Wouldn't it be cool if X?
  • Someone needs to do X.
  • Why X was a mistake.
  • Why X looks like a mistake, but isn't.
  • What it's like to do X.
  • Here's a useful technique that worked.
  • Here's a technique I thought would be useful but didn't work.
  • Why thing X sucks.
  • Comparison of similar things X and Y.
  • Why we should be paying more attention to X.

source

Lightning Talks Slides

Use less words, more visuals.

Don't plan on spending much, if any,
time on your intro or closing slides.

Use your knowledge of learning styles to make slides
as accessible and as easily parsed as possible.

Teach for the middle.
