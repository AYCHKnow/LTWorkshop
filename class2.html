7 Learning Styles Visual

Prefers to use images, colors, charts,
and spacial cues.

Replace words with images

Use colors to highlight

7 Learning Styles Aural

Prefers to use sound, rhythms, rhymes,
and auditory cues.

These people have the strongest memories

Use sound recordings

Play a "pump up" song

Use mnemonics
ie. Never Eat Soggy Waffles

7 Learning Styles Verbal

Prefers to use words, both spoken and written,
and linguistic cues.

This is the most common teaching method

Use role playing, active discussions, recordings.

Use self talk (rubber ducking), mnemonics, and teaching others

7 Learning Styles Physical

Prefers to use hands, body, sense of touch,
and kinesthetic cues.

Discuss sensations and physical feelings.

Use objects and role-playing

Talk (teach) with your hands

Active rather than passive - do something

7 Learning Styles Logical

Prefers to use logic, reasoning, sequences,
and mathematical cues.

Progress from smaller pieces to the big picture.

Use diagrams, structure, and organization.

7 Learning Styles Social

Prefers to learn along with others.
Works in combination with other learning styles.

Use group activities.

Have students explain understanding to group.

7 Learning Styles Solitary

Prefers to learn on their own.
Works in combination with other learning styles.

Use self-study.

7 Learning Styles Combinations

Tips for Getting Started

Teach for the middle.

Welcome questions and participation.

Emphasize hands-on learning.

Be humble and eliminate fear.

You don't know everything, and that's ok.
You're level of expertise should feel obtainable with a bit of hard work.

Avoid making assumptions and feigning surprise.

Complex subjects are HARD, but also learnable.
Use careful language. Follow Hacker School's Social Rules

It's ok not to have an answer.

Talk through your process, Google stuff

Exercise: Pairs & Shapes

We're going to practice giving instructions.

Why we're doing this exercise
  • It mimicks what we do when we teach
  • Forces us to communicate with words, not visuals
  • We can see if our communication was successful

Breakout Into Pairs

Ideally, with someone you haven't met before today.

Exercise : How to Play

Teacher

  • Pick up the first printout.
  • Instruct the students to assemble the pieces to match what you see on the printout.
  • No peeking!
  • Ask questions.
  • Decide when to stop the "lesson".

Student

  • Listen to the instructor and assemble the shapes.
  • No peeking!
  • Do not show the teacher what you are working on.
  • Ask questions.

Exercise : Takeaways

  • What were the challenges?
  • How could you tell if the student "got it"?
  • What did you do when the student didn't get it?
  • Who asked the most questions, student or teacher?
